Intel

Figure 5: The top contract foundry companies based on market share.
Source: Adapted from [12]

Last sits Intel, the once prominent U.S. based foundry. They have had a long history of being at the head of the pack when it comes to producing the smallest, most powerful circuits. Over the past few years, they have been failing to keep pace with TSMC and Samsung. A key piece to falling behind was losing its crucial partnership with Apple with producing the processors inside of the Macbook. Not being able to meet revenue goals, an invested hedge fund is pushing the company to “explore strategic alternatives“ [8]. This stopped the company from progressing past the 10nm mark, generations before Samsung and TSMC. Currently, they have plans to enter the market again to be a true competitor in the fabrication space, but keeping up in the race requires hundreds of billions of dollars of commitment to keep on the frontier. This forces the corporation to rely in part on TSMC and Samsung to manufacture components in the laptops with the famous “Intel Inside“ logo stickered on. It is still up in the air if this can be enough for the company to make the comeback, if the challenge proves too tough the United States will be forced to import all high end semiconductors.
