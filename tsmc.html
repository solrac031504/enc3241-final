TSMC


One of the largest of these firms is a foundry that supports the development of most PCs and smartphones around the world: the Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company. When people think about the computers they by, they mostly think of the front-facing companies that you purchase from, e.g. Apple, Dell, HP. They mostly focus on the R&D process of the value chain, but at most participate in the ATP phase of the manufacturing process. These companies rely on TSMC for semiconductor fabrication, sometimes as their sole provider. For example, Apple’s newest A16 chip at the heart of the iPhone 15 is designed by Apple, but relies entirely on TSMC on fabrication. The production of the iPhone, like many other high-computation devices, would not be possible without TMSC. The firm is looking to diversify their fabrication to the United States and Europe including the 40 billion dollar fab investment mentioned earlier.
